MATHEMATICAL PRACTICE STANDARDS

Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.

Desiree is making a flag in the shape of a triangle for a parade float.

To fit on the float, the area of the flag can be no more than 600 square inches.

She has decided to make the height of the flag equal to \(3 \frac{1}{2}\) feet.

Write an inequality to represent possible lengths for the base of the flag.

\(b \leq 28.571428\overline{57}\)

When students are solving inequalities, they need to be able to interpret the solution.

Mathematically, the solution above tells us that the base can be any length \(\leq 28.571428\overline{57}\) ; however, in the real world, it is not likely that someone would measure to this degree of precision.

It is more likely that this person would measure to the nearest inch or half inch.

In the problem above, students might reason that Desiree would make the base of the flag 28 inches or 28.5 inches.

In similar problems, the quantities being considered are whole objects, such as people.

Students take this into consideration when considering solutions to the problem.
