Concepts and Skills to Master:

By the end of this section, students should be able to:

  • Write different but equivalent numeric expressions to represent a real-world problem using grouping and operator symbols correctly (parentheses, fraction bars, middle dot, etc.).
  • Determine whether two or more numeric expressions are equivalent by correctly simplifying the expressions (follow order of operations, recognize and interpret grouping symbols and operator symbols, etc.)
  • Determine whether two or more algebraic expressions are equivalent by evaluating the expressions for specific values of the variable.
  • Translate the word form of an algebraic expression to its symbolic representation and vice versa.
  • Use and understand academic vocabulary.
  • Recognize and interpret patterns and structure in numeric expressions and use this understanding to write algebraic expressions.
